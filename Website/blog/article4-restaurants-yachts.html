Celebrations

Best Restaurants, Yachts and Experiences for Celebrations in Koh Samui

← Back to Journal

Koh Samui is an island that lends itself to celebration. For groups marking something that matters - a milestone birthday, an anniversary, a long-awaited reunion - the setting, the privacy, and the range of experiences make it an easy place to get right.

Here are some of our recommendations - restaurants worth booking, yacht options worth considering, and experiences that tend to make a trip memorable.

Private Dining at the Villa

For many guests, the best dinner of the trip happens right here. A private chef, the terrace, an infinity pool lit up at dusk - some evenings, there's nowhere else you'd rather be. Our team arranges private chef dinners regularly - Thai menus, international menus, or a mix of both, served on the terrace with the lights of Chaweng Bay spread out below.

For a celebration dinner, this is usually the first recommendation. It's intimate, personal, and completely tailored to the group.

Restaurants Worth Booking

For evenings out, Koh Samui has a strong restaurant scene — particularly around Bophut, Chaweng, and the northern coast. A few worth knowing:

  • Fisherman's Village, Bophut — ten minutes from the villa. A charming strip of waterfront restaurants, tuk-tuk street food stalls, and boutique bars. Best for a relaxed group evening. On Friday nights there's a walking street market that takes over the main road.
  • Koh Thai Kitchen — one of the island's most-loved Thai restaurants, known for its authentic cooking and warm service. A local staple rather than a tourist stop.
  • The Larder — casual European food with a strong brunch and lunch offering. Good for a mid-trip morning out.
  • Dining on the Rocks, Six Senses — for a special night out, the Six Senses resort restaurant offers elevated food with dramatic clifftop views. Worth the occasion.
  • Prego, Anantara — reliable Italian in a beautiful resort setting, good for groups who want something more formal without going too far.

Booking in advance is advisable for any of the above, particularly during peak season (December–April).

Yacht and Boat Charters

A day on the water is one of the defining experiences of a Koh Samui stay — and for a celebration group, a private charter is the way to do it.

Options range from longtail boats for short island-hopping trips to fully crewed luxury yachts for full-day charters. The most popular routes:

  • Ang Thong Marine National Park — 42 uninhabited islands about 30km from Samui. Most full-day charters include snorkelling, kayaking through the emerald lagoons, and a hike to the viewpoint. One of the best days available in Southeast Asia.
  • Koh Tao and Koh Nang Yuan — the dive sites around Koh Tao are world-class. The twin-peaked Nang Yuan island is one of the most photographed spots in the Gulf of Thailand.
  • Sunset cruise — a shorter half-day option, typically departing late afternoon with champagne and canapés. Ideal for a birthday evening that starts on the water and ends at dinner.

We can arrange yacht charters for guests — from a simple longtail to a fully crewed catamaran. Let us know the date, group size and what you have in mind when you enquire.

Experiences That Make a Celebration

Beyond dining and boats, a few experiences tend to stand out for celebration groups:

  • In-villa spa morning — therapists come to the villa for group massages and treatments. A popular choice for birthdays and hen parties alike. No transfers, no timing stress.
  • Thai cooking class — a morning activity that works for every group. You cook, you eat, you leave with recipes. Usually done in a local home kitchen.
  • Muay Thai session — for groups who want something active. A private session with a trainer at one of the island's gyms, followed by a recovery swim at the villa.
  • Elephant sanctuary visit — an ethical sanctuary where elephants roam freely. Genuinely moving and suitable for all ages. Worth booking early as spaces are limited.
  • Cocktail masterclass — we can arrange a mixologist to come to the villa for a private cocktail class, which tends to become an evening in itself.
  • Beach clubs — Koh Samui has a small but well-curated beach club scene. Karma Beach (Bophut) is boho-chic with an infinity pool and live music evenings. Chi Samui (Bangrak) is the most high-end option - a 25-metre saltwater pool, international DJs, and a serious food and wine menu. Tembo Beach Club (Bangrak) suits a long afternoon that rolls into the evening. Cabanas (Chaweng) offers ocean-facing pools and sunset jazz. Seen Beach Club (Chaweng Noi) is a rooftop-style venue with pool access, a DJ lineup, and views over the bay - good for a full evening out. Worth booking ahead for larger groups.
